CASE STUDY PRESENTATION EVALUATION RUBRIC

This rubric evaluates the quality and completeness of a case study presentation.

EVALUATION CRITERIA:

1. **Presentation Quality** (Weight: 10%)
   Is the presentation professional in the use of templates, colours, slide designs and presentable to a prospective client?

2. **Presentation Structure** (Weight: 10%)
   Is the presentation structured into logical sections with continuity and flow? Is the analysis structured around a framework (Porter's 5 Forces, BCG matrix, etc.)?

3. **Story Telling Skills** (Weight: 10%)
   Is there a clear beginning, middle and end to the case study analysis with a coherent story throughout the presentation?

4. **Case Study Solution** (Weight: 60% total)

   4a. **Criteria for Selection** (Weight: 5%)
   Has the problem statement been clearly articulated?

   4b. **Opportunity Sizing** (Weight: 10%)
   Have value pools been identified with clear opportunity sizing/business case?

   4c. **Depth of Research** (Weight: 10%)
   Has research been conducted across the 3 Lenses â€“ Customer, Market and Capability perspectives with clear outcomes?

   4d. **Current Business Model definition** (Weight: 10%)
   Has the existing Operating Model been explored in detail and is there proper analysis conducted to identify the challenges and gaps?

   4e. **Business Model Analysis** (Weight: 5%)
   Is there a proper analysis conducted to identify the challenges and gaps?

   4f. **Target Business Model Alternatives** (Weight: 10%)
   Has the candidate shown creativity and business acumen to arrive at multiple options of the target business model?

   4g. **Criteria for Selection** (Weight: 5%)
   Has an evaluation/selection framework been created with clear criteria to select the best alternative?

   4h. **GTM approach for Target Business Model** (Weight: 5%)
   Is there a clear articulation of the Go To Market proposition for the Target Business Model?

   4i. **Translation of Business Model into Product Vision & Roadmap** (Weight: 5%)
   Has the business model been broken down into product features and capabilities with a vision & roadmap, including an MVP? Have we considered the customer personas, user journeys and applied the Design Thinking lenses of Desirability, Viability, Feasibility and Sustainability?

   4j. **Implementation plan** (Weight: 5%)
   Has an implementation plan been created with clear timelines and team/pod structures to manage delivery? Any references to the Lean Agile/SAFe methodology is an added bonus.

5. **Others** (Weight: 10%)
   Any other considerations well articulated?

EVALUATION SCALE:

5 = Exceptional - Far exceeds expectations, outstanding quality
4 = Strong - Clearly meets expectations, high quality
3 = Adequate - Meets basic expectations, acceptable quality
2 = Weak - Below expectations, significant gaps
1 = Poor - Far below expectations, major deficiencies
0 = Absent - Not addressed at all

CRITICAL CRITERIA:
- Problem Statement (4a) - Must score 4+
- Business Model Alternatives (4f) - Must score 4+
- All sub-criteria in Case Study Solution should average 3.5+

RECOMMENDATION RULES:
- PASS: overall_score >= 3.5 AND all critical criteria >= 4
- BORDERLINE: overall_score >= 3.0 OR 1 critical criterion below 4
- FAIL: overall_score < 3.0 OR 2+ critical criteria below 4
