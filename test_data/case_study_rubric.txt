CASE STUDY PRESENTATION EVALUATION RUBRIC

This rubric evaluates the quality and completeness of a case study presentation delivered by a candidate.

CRITICAL CRITERIA (Must meet all to pass):

1. **Case Study Solution - Problem Statement** (CRITICAL - Weight: 15%)
   Clear articulation of the problem being solved.
   - Has the problem statement been clearly articulated?
   - Is it specific and well-defined?
   - Does it frame the challenge appropriately?

2. **Business Model Analysis** (CRITICAL - Weight: 20%)
   Comprehensive analysis of current and target business models.
   - Has the existing Operating Model been explored in detail?
   - Is there proper analysis to identify challenges and gaps?
   - Have multiple target business model alternatives been proposed?
   - Is there creativity and business acumen in the alternatives?

IMPORTANT CRITERIA:

3. **Presentation Quality** (Weight: 10%)
   Professional presentation using appropriate templates and design.
   - Is the presentation professional in use of templates, colors, slide designs?
   - Is it presentable to a prospective client?

4. **Presentation Structure** (Weight: 10%)
   Logical organization with clear flow and continuity.
   - Is the presentation structured into logical sections?
   - Is there continuity and flow?
   - Is analysis structured around a framework (Porter's 5 Forces, BCG matrix, etc.)?

5. **Story Telling Skills** (Weight: 10%)
   Clear narrative with beginning, middle, and end.
   - Is there a clear beginning, middle, and end?
   - Is there a coherent story throughout the presentation?

6. **Opportunity Sizing** (Weight: 10%)
   Clear business case with value pools identified.
   - Have value pools been identified?
   - Is there clear opportunity sizing/business case?

7. **Depth of Research** (Weight: 10%)
   Research across multiple perspectives.
   - Has research been conducted across Customer, Market, and Capability perspectives?
   - Are there clear outcomes from research?

8. **Selection Framework** (Weight: 5%)
   Criteria for evaluating alternatives.
   - Has an evaluation/selection framework been created?
   - Are there clear criteria to select the best alternative?

9. **GTM Approach** (Weight: 5%)
   Go-to-market strategy for target business model.
   - Is there clear articulation of Go To Market proposition?

10. **Product Vision & Roadmap** (Weight: 5%)
    Translation of business model into product features.
    - Has business model been broken down into product features and capabilities?
    - Is there a vision & roadmap, including MVP?
    - Have customer personas and user journeys been considered?
    - Have Desirability, Viability, Feasibility, Sustainability been addressed?

11. **Implementation Plan** (Weight: 5%)
    Clear execution plan with timelines.
    - Has an implementation plan been created with clear timelines?
    - Are team/pod structures defined for delivery?
    - Any references to Lean Agile/SAFe methodology?

EVALUATION SCALE:

5 = Exceptional - Beyond expectations, creative and thorough
4 = Strong - Clearly meets requirements, well-executed
3 = Adequate - Meets basic requirements but lacks depth
2 = Weak - Incomplete or poorly executed
1 = Poor - Missing or fundamentally flawed
0 = Absent - Not addressed at all

RECOMMENDATION RULES:

- PASS: overall_score >= 3.5 AND all critical criteria >= 4
- BORDERLINE: overall_score >= 3.0 OR 1 critical failure
- FAIL: overall_score < 3.0 OR 2+ critical failures
